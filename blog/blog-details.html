Student Visa 2026: What Changed for USA, UK, Canada and Australia and What Every Indian Student Must Know

Date Released

Category

If you are planning to study in 2026 you should know that the rules have changed a lot more than most students think. This year the USA, UK, Canada and Australia have made changes to their visa policies, increased the amount of money students need to have, set new limits on visas and are checking everything more closely. Students who are well prepared and really want to study are still getting in. Students who are not prepared are seeing their applications rejected more often than before. Here is a clear overview of what's happening in each country.

USA F1 Visa: What the 2026 Policy Shift Means

The Institute of International Education says that India is still the largest source of international students in the USA with around 3,37,630 students enrolled in 2024. However the number of international students dropped by 7 percent in late 2025 and graduate enrollments fell by 15 percent because of uncertainty about immigration.

The F1 visa allows students to study full time, work on campus during semesters and do Optional Practical Training for 12 months after graduation, which can be extended to 36 months for STEM courses. EB2 and EB3 are still paths to getting permanent residence.

The big change is that the rules are being enforced strictly. If you do not have proof of money, your study goals are not clear, or you do a poor job in your visa interview you are more likely to be rejected. So it is very important to be prepared.

UK Graduate Route: Intact But Compliance Is Stricter

The UK Student Visa is processed in 3 weeks, which is one of the fastest options. The 2 year Graduate Route is still available. It has been extended to 3 years for PhD graduates while the Skilled Worker Visa can lead to settlement.

In 2026 the rules are stricter. Universities have to track attendance and report if students are not complying with the rules. If you miss classes it can affect your visa. The requirements for IELTS have increased for courses with scores needing to be around 6.5 to 7.0.

Canada: The Most Significant Policy Changes

Canada has made significant changes to its policies for 2026. The Student Direct Stream has been discontinued. All students now have to follow the regular study permit process, which takes between 4 to 12 weeks.

There is a limit on study permits with only around 1,55,000 available for 2026 but masters students are not affected. The amount of money students need to have has increased, with students needing to show around CAD 22,895 (which's around â‚¹14-15 lakhs) for living expenses. Some graduates of colleges may not qualify for a Post Graduation Work Permit. The requirements for English have also changed, with students needing a CLB 7 for degree programs and CLB 5 for diplomas.

Express Entry is still a path to getting permanent residence for international students, making Canada an attractive option for those seeking long-term settlement opportunities.

Canada 2026 Requirements Checklist

  • Financial proof: CAD 22,895 per year
  • Processing time: 4-12 weeks (regular stream)
  • English requirements: CLB 5-7
  • Express Entry pathway available

Australia: Higher Costs and Stricter Checks

Australia has introduced a Genuine Student test, which focuses on whether students intend to study. Financial documents are being checked closely including where the money is coming from.

The costs have increased, with the Subclass 500 visa fee around AU$2,000 (which is around â‚¹1.1 lakh) making it one of the highest in the world. The fees for post study work visas have also increased in 2026.

However Australia is planning to allow around 2,95,000 students in 2026, which shows that there is still a lot of demand. Students can get post study work visas for 2 to 4 years depending on their course and location. Skilled Migration is still a path to getting permanent residence.

The One Thing All Four Countries Agree On

No major country's closing its doors to international students. They are all making the rules stricter to filter out students who're not genuine while still accepting students who are serious and prepared. The standard is higher now so in 2026 it is more important than ever to be well prepared.

"The standard is higher now. Success requires genuine intent, proper preparation, and complete documentation. Countries want committed students who will contribute to their education systems."
