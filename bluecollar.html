Our Process

How to Get Your Overseas Job — 7 Steps

From document submission to landing abroad - here is exactly how it works with Swasthavritti, Hyderabad.

Step 01
Document Submission

Visit our Suchitra Circle office or send documents online. Required: resume, SSC & intermediate certificates, PCC, medical certificate, passport copies, experience letters (if any) and a passport-size photo.

Step 02
Invoice & Employment Agreement

On the same day your documents are approved, we generate your employment invoice and agreement. No hidden fees — everything is transparent upfront.

Step 03
Job Offer Letter

You receive a formal job offer letter from our employer network within 10 business days of document approval. Role, salary, benefits and contract duration are all specified.

Step 04
Contract & Certificate of Sponsorship

Employer confirms the contract and issues the Certificate of Sponsorship (COS), Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) or equivalent sponsorship document required for the visa.

Step 05
Credential Assessment

Your educational and professional qualifications are assessed and verified by the relevant authority in the destination country. We manage all correspondence and translations.

Step 06
Immigration Profile & Visa Application

We submit your immigration profile to the destination portal. PCC, medical exam, biometrics and passport copy are collected. We track the application and respond to any requests for additional information.

Step 07
Visa Approval & Travel

Once approved, we coordinate travel arrangements and conduct a pre-departure briefing covering accommodation, banking, local transport, workplace culture and emergency contacts in your destination country.

Family

Can I Bring My Family Abroad?

Dependent Family Sponsorship

Yes. Most destination countries allow workers on skilled worker visas to sponsor dependent family members (spouse and children) after settling in.

Swasthavritti assists with dependent visa applications as an add-on service.

Permanent Residency Pathway

After 5 years of continuous employment, most workers become eligible for Permanent Residency (PR) - allowing you to live and work permanently in the destination country without a visa.

Our team guides you through the PR application when the time comes.
